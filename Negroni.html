Italy | Negroni

It is thought that the drink was invented in 1919 in Florence Italy at Caff√® Casoni. Count Camillo Negroni asked his friend and bartender Fosco Scarselli, to strengthen his favourite cocktail, the Americano. Scarselli did so by switching out the soda for gin and garnished the drink with an orange peel to signify it was a different drink. Soon after that everyone was ordering the "Count Negroni" and the drink has continued to grow in popularity ever since.

  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Garnish: Slice of orange
  • Glass: Old Fashioned Glass

Ingredients:

  • 25ml Gin
  • 25ml Sweet Vermouth
  • 25ml Campari
  • Ice

Method:

Add gin, vermouth and Campari into a mixing glass with ice. Stir until the outside of the glass feels cold. Strain into your chosen glass and add ice. Garnish with an orange slice.
