Argentina | Fernet & Coke

The Combination is known locally as Fernandito and was invented by a local drummer called Oscar "el Negro" Becerra in the 1970s. Fernet was originally mixed with soda water before coke, however one day Becerra had no soda water and so added Coke instead. The sweetnes of the Coke counteracted the Fernet's bitterness creating a paletable drink. Although it wasn't until the 1990s that the drink started grow in popularity. Between 1990 and 2013 Argentinas annual output of Fernet went from 1 million gallons to nearly 15 million, with a 405% increase between 2004 and 2015. Argentina now consumes nearly three-quaters of the worlds Fernet.

  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Garnish: Lemon wedge
  • Glass: Tall Glass

Ingredients:

  • 240ml Coke
  • 120ml Fernet
  • Ice

Method:

Fill your chosen glass with ice, add the Coke and Fernet and stir gently. Garnish with lemon wedge.
