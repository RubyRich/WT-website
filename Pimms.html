England | Pimms

The creator of Pimms was the owner of a London oyster bar, called James Pimm. The drink was invented sometime between 1823 and 1840, the original version features gin, quinine and various herbs and reportedly helped with digestion. The first name it was known by was the "House Cup" which then later later inspired the name "Pimms No. 1 cup" and then was shortened to Pimms Cup. Over the years 5 more cups where made, each with a different alcohol base: No. 2, with scotch whisky; No. 3, brandy; No. 4, rum; No. 5, rye whiskey; and No. 6, vodka. The drink is the signature drink of the Wimbledon Tennis Tournament where the first Pimms bar opened in 1971.

  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Garnish: Mint leaves
  • Glass: High Ball Glass

Ingredients:

  • 200ml Pimms No. 1
  • 600ml Lemonade
  • Mint leaves
  • Sliced cucumber
  • Sliced orange
  • 400g Sliced strawberries
  • Ice

Method:

Fill jug with ice, add the Pimms and lemonade. Stir mixture then add mint, cucumber and fruit. Garnish with mint leaves.
