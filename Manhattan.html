USA - New York | Manhattan

The most popular story for the creation of the Manhattan is that the recipe was created by Dr. Iain Marshal in the early 1880's. It was for a party hosted by Lady Randolph Churchill, the mother of Winston Chruchill. Because the party was held in the Manhattan Club in New York the drink was therefore named after it. However this story was soon pronounced a myth as at the time of the "party" Lady Churchill was pregnant in England. A more plausible story surfaced in 1923, it stated that a bartender at New Yorks' Hoffman House recounted that the cocktail was created by a man named Black who kept a place ten doors below Houston Street on Broadway.

  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Garnish: Strip of orange zest
  • Glass: Martini Glass

Ingredients:

  • 75ml Bourbon
  • 15ml Sweet vermouth
  • 15ml Extra dry vermouth
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 5ml Cherry Eau De Vie
  • Ice

Method:

Add all ingredients into a mixing glass, add ice and stir. When the desired dilution has been achieved, strain into chosen glass. Garnish with a strip of orange zest.
