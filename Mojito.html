Cuba | Mojito

In the 1500's, Sir Francis Drake landed in Havana to sack the city for its gold, however the sacking was unsuccessful. At the time his crew where suffering from scurvy and dysentery. A small party went ashore to Cuba and came back with ingredients for a medicine created by local South American Indians as they where known for there remidies for many diseases. They used Aguarediente de Cana, mint leaves and the juices from sugar cane and lime put together with a tonic which turned out effective.

  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Garnish: Mint leaves, Lime wedge
  • Glass: Tall Glass

Ingredients:

  • 60ml White rum
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1tsp Granulated sugar
  • A small handful of mint leaves
  • Soda water

Method:

In a jug mix the lime juice, sugar and mint leaves, crushing the mint, this can be done with a large spoon or rolling pin. Pour mix over rum with a long handeled spoon into your chosen glass with ice. Finish with soda water and garnish with mint.
